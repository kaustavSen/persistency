Persistency analysis

Persistency measures how effective an insurance company has been in retaining its policyholders. Here we have analyzed the persistency experience of life insurance companies over the past years.

13th month persistency

The 13th month persistency rate measures the proportion of policyholders who end up paying their premium for the second policy year. If a policyholder feels that the insurance purchased is not a good fit and was mis-sold to then, he/she may simply lapse their policy after paying the initial premium for the first year. This is quite a popular KPI used in the insurance sector.

Listed insurers have maintained a healthy 13th month persistency rate of generally above 70%. However, Pramerica Life has shown the greatest improvement over the past 5 years.

Next, if we look at the change in percentage points (pp) over the same period as above, we observe the law of diminishing returns kick in. It is much easier to increase the persistency rate by 20pp from 40% to 60% but comparatively harder to get a 10pp increase from 70% to 80%.
